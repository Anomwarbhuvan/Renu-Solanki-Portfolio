# Winters in India: Top 5 Hill Stations to Visit

Winters are approaching the diverse landscape of India and soon many hill stations in India will be covered with snow making it a perfect time to witness snowfall. With lush green valleys, snow-capped mountains, evergreen forests, and whatnot! India has the perfect places for snow lovers and adventure seekers to try out various snow sports activities.  

In this article, we have listed some incredible destinations you can explore on your next visit. Add them to your bucket list and experience the snowfall.  

## Top 5 Hill Stations in India to Visit This Winter

### 1. Gulmarg, Jammu & Kashmir

Gulmarg is a hill station located in the Pir Panjal range of the union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. The serenity of this place attracts tourists from all over the world. The snow-covered Himalayas, lush green valleys draped in pristine white, fun activities in the snow, etc., are a few things that attract tourists to Gulmarg. December to mid-February is the best time to witness snowfall.  

**Do you know:** It has also served as a popular destination for many Bollywood movies like *Haider*, *Highway*, *Bajrangi Bhaijaan*, etc.  

**Things to do:**  
- Gulmarg Gondola: the world’s second-longest and second-highest cable car project offering magnificent views of Nanda Devi, LOC, and the Pir Panjal range.  
- Snow sports: Trekking, skiing, snowboarding, and mountaineering courses at The Indian Institute of Skiing and Mountaineering.  
- Drung Waterfall: Freezes completely in winter.  
- Frozen Alpathar Lake: Walk on the hard frozen surface.  
- Other attractions: Strawberry Valley, Apharwat Peak, St. Mary’s Church, Tangmarg town, Baba Reshi Shrine, Ningle Nallah, Baderkoot, Gogaldara.  
- Gulmarg Biosphere Reserve: Home to exotic species; spot a snow leopard or musk deer if lucky.  

### 2. Kufri, Himachal Pradesh

If you are a photo enthusiast and love to capture snow shots, Kufri is the ideal place for you. Click as many pictures as you can while making memories for life. This place offers mesmerizing views that leave tourists always wanting more.  

Located 20 km from Shimla, winters in Kufri start in October and go till March (sometimes April). December to February is the best time to witness snowfall.  

**Things to do:**  
- Ski slopes and winter sports festival in February.  
- Tobogganing and snow slides.  
- Visit Fagu Valley with apple orchards and cedar trees; enjoy yak rides.  
- Kufri Fun World: One of the highest amusement parks in the world offering go-karting, tunnel rides, zip-lining, etc.  

### 3. Auli, Uttarakhand

Auli is a Himalayan ski resort and hill station in the Chamoli district. December to February is perfect for snowfall.  

**Things to do:**  
- Skiing: Auli is known as the ski capital of India.  
- Ropeway rides and visit to the world’s highest man-made lake.  
- Snow-sports: Snowboarding, snow motorbiking, skating.  
- Trekking: Auli – Gorson Bugyal trek for breathtaking views.  
- Scenic views of mountains: Nanda Devi, Kamet, Dunagiri, Mana Parvat.  
- Nearby attractions: Badrinath Temple, Valley of Flowers, Hem Kumb Sahib Temple.  

### 4. Patnitop, Jammu & Kashmir

Patnitop is a hill station in the Udhampur district that transforms into a fairyland during winter (November to February).  

**Things to do:**  
- Winter Carnival: Three-day festival hosted by Jammu & Kashmir Tourism Department.  
- Snowboarding, motorbiking, winter camping.  
- Visit Sudh Mahadev and Billoo ki Powri.  
- Skiing on thick layers of snow.  

### 5. Sethan Valley, Himachal Pradesh

This Buddhist village is located 12 km from Manali. During winter, it gets covered in snow, making it perfect for skiing, snow hiking, and snowboarding.  

**Things to do:**  
- Igloo stays with hot coffee.  
- Bouldering on granite and gneiss rocks.  
- Trek to Indrasana Peak, the throne of God Indra.  
- Visit Pandu Ropa: Mythological site where Pandavas resided.  
- Stargazing in the peaceful valley.  

There are many more places to witness snowfall in India such as Manali, Dharamshala, Shimla, Dalhousie, Mcleodganj, Mussorie, etc. Winters are chilly, so dress in woolens, heavy boots, jackets, and stay hydrated with hot beverages.  

India’s hill stations are a paradise in winter, with snow, breezy winds, and serene surroundings. Witness snowfall, build a snowman, or enjoy a hot meal—the choice is yours. Plan your next visit and experience the ethereal beauty of these destinations.
