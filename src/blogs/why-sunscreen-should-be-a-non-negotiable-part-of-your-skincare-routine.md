part of everyday skincare. No SPF, no glow, and no show! Often one of the most overlooked skincare products, sunscreen protects you from premature aging, pigmentation, and even skin cancer. As seasons change, so should your sunscreen routine, depending on your skin type. If you want to know the many benefits of sunscreen, stick with us till the end to master the sunscreen tutorial.

## What is Sunscreen?

Sunscreen is a product that acts as a shield between your skin and harmful UV rays, pollutants, dust, and sunburn. Ultraviolet rays emitted by the sun can severely damage your skin and even lead to skin cancer. With the right protection, you can maintain flawless skin without worrying about damage.

UV rays are known to have a long-term impact on the skin and can slowly lead to aging. Those wrinkles appearing before you turned 30? Thank UVA rays, they can make you look like 40 while you are still in your 30s. Like Joey had a deal with God, not everyone does, and often we end up saying, “Why, God, why? We had a deal.” The truth is, we do not. So the best way to protect your skin is by understanding sunscreens and how they can help your skin type. Before we jump into the benefits, let us take a quick look at the different types of UV rays.

1. **UVA Rays -** Premature aging and wrinkles under your eyes? Blame UVA rays. They penetrate deep into your skin, making it prone to early aging and sagging.
2. **UVB Rays -** Unlike UVA rays, UVB rays work on the surface level and damage the exterior portion of your skin. Sunburn is one of the most common effects of UVB rays while it can also lead to more severe issues such as skin cancer. Unlike UVA rays, UVB rays affect the surface of the skin. Sunburn is the most common effect, and prolonged exposure can lead to more severe issues such as skin cancer.

That’s when sunscreens become your saviour, it absorbs both UVA and UVB rays before the damage is done. The formulation of sunscreen enables it to reflect the harmful rays easily while having a soft texture on the skin.

## Top 5 Benefits of Wearing Sunscreen Daily

Sun damage doesn’t knock before it enters, sunscreen is your guard. Think of sunscreen as your daily invisible armor that protects you like a bodyguard.

### 1. Lowers the Risk of Skin Cancer

Let's accept that we can’t stay indoors during the day. In fact the early morning sun rays too contain UV rays that can lead to skin cancer. This doesn’t mean you can ignore the sun completely or will not step out at all. But, you can lower the risk of skin cancer with continuous use of sunscreen, it drastically reduces the risk offering a protection layer on the skin.

### 2. Prevents Sunburn

Whether you tie a heavy cloth across your face or wear a helmet while riding a bike, you will not be spared by the dangerous UV rays. Continuous exposure to the sun often leads to red patches on the face or body, or even results in discoloration, giving you a pigmentation for a lifetime.

### 3. Slows Down Premature Aging

UVA rays easily damage the elastin and collagen present under the skin, affecting the physical layer. This leads to premature ageing, visible wrinkles, and fine lines. When proteins are attacked, skin loses its protection agents leaving it prone to damages.

### 4. Reduces the Risk of Hyperpigmentation and Melasma

Sun exposure often worsens conditions like Melasma and dark spots, hyperpigmentation is noted in all skin types and can leave permanent spots if not taken care of on time. Hence, it is highly advisable to wear sunscreen regardless of your skin tone or color.

### 5. Boosts Skin Hydration

One of the best uses of a sunscreen is it keeps the skin hydrated and helps keep the skin barrier intact. Sunscreens are often loaded with hydrating ingredients like Hyaluronic acid and ceramides to keep the moisture locked in.

## How to Choose the Right Sunscreen for Your Skin

Now that you know the importance of sunscreen, let’s help you in figuring out the right sun block for your skin. Sunscreen often comes in different formulations such as lotion, spray, gel-based, or cream. Sunscreen formulations are majorly categorised into two types - chemical sunscreen and mineral sunscreen.

It is always advised to use a sunscreen containing SPF (sun protection factor). The higher the SPF, the more protection it provides. A few things to remember while selecting a sunscreen are -

1. Consult a dermatologist to understand your skin type or address any specific skin issues.

2. Choose a higher SPF, typically SPF 30 or above, for better protection.

3. Dermatologists recommend a broad-spectrum sunscreen as it gives protections against UVA and UVB rays.

4. Match sunscreen to your skin type: gel-based for oily skin, cream-based for dry skin.

Sunscreen is your first line of defense.

Think of it as investing in your future self because glowing, protected skin is always in style.
