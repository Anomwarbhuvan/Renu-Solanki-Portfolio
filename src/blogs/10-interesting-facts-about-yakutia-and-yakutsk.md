Yakutsk is the capital city of Yakutia and is the coldest city on earth with winter temperatures dropping below -70°C.

Yakutia, also known as the Sakha Republic, is the largest country subdivision of Russia. It is located at a distance of 8,300 km from Moscow by road. Yakutsk is the capital city of Yakutia and is the coldest city on earth. 40% of the area of the region is covered by the Arctic Ocean.

Let’s explore some interesting facts about Yakutia and Yakutsk.

This picturesque land of Yakutia is still undiscovered and unexplored and is sometimes considered a mysterious place on the planet. Yakutia is famous in the world for its winter season which lasts from October to May.

Yakutia is also known as the Land of the Rivers for having over seven hundred thousand rivers and around eight hundred thousand lakes. Some of the most famous rivers are the Lena, Viluy, Olenek, Aldan, Kolyma, Indigirka, Olekma, Anabar, and Yana.

## Natural Resources and Economy
The famous mining industry of Yakutia has made its name in the world. Natural gas, oil, diamonds, gold, coal, and several precious metals are mined here. The economy also depends on wood processing, agriculture products, and meat processing. In recent years, tourism has also started growing rapidly.

As there is a lack of road connectivity, Yakutia is well connected by airways with more than 30 airports, making travel easier for its widely scattered population.

Yakutia is also known for its well-preserved natural habitat, rich in flora and fauna, attracting visitors from across the globe.

## About Yakutsk
Yakutsk is a rapidly developing city mainly driven by the mining industry. It has two airports—Yakutsk Airport and Magan Airport.

The temperature of this region remains extremely low throughout the year. The average temperature is around –8°C, while winters can drop to –64°C.

Yakutsk was founded in 1632 by the Yakuts who migrated from different parts of Siberia and later integrated with the indigenous communities. The Yakuts are a Turkic ethnic group, also known as the Sakha people.

## 10 Interesting Facts About Yakutia and Yakutsk

### World Mammoth Museum
The Yakut paleontologist Petr Alekseyevich Lazarev initiated the establishment of the World Mammoth Museum in 1991. It serves as a scientific and cultural center for studying mammoth fauna and their habitat. In 1995, it was renamed the Mammoth Museum.

Around 75% of the world’s mammoth remains are found in Yakutia. Earlier, these fossils were sent to institutions in Moscow, St. Petersburg, and Novosibirsk.

### Lena Pillar Nature Park
Lena Pillar Nature Park features spectacular rock pillars ranging from 100 to 300 meters in height. It is located along the banks of the Lena River.

The pillars were formed over many years due to extreme climatic conditions. The park was established in 1995 and became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2012.

### Population of Yakutsk
Yakutsk has a relatively small population for such a vast region. As per the 2021 census, around 350,000 people live here, spread across different parts of the city.

### Stroganina – A Raw Fish Dish
Stroganina is a traditional frozen raw fish dish popular in Russia. During harsh winters when farming is not possible, people rely on frozen fish from Arctic rivers for food.

### Unty – Traditional Footwear
Unty are traditional fur boots worn by the Evenks, the indigenous people of North Russia. These boots are made from reindeer hides and decorated with Arctic fox fur or beads.

> Unty are perfectly designed for surviving the extreme cold of Yakutia.

### Locals Prefer the Name “Sakha”
The Yakut people prefer to be called “Sakha,” as it reflects their cultural identity. They migrated to Yakutia between the 13th and 14th centuries.

### Rich in Natural Resources
Yakutia produces around 15% of the world’s diamonds. It is also rich in oil, gas, gold, and other minerals, making it a key mining region.

### Culture and Traditions
Yakutia is known for its diverse culture and traditions. The Yakut language, of Turkic origin, is widely spoken alongside Russian.

The most important cultural event is the summer festival “Ysyach,” celebrated on the longest day of the year. It lasts for two to three days and is a major social gathering.

### Extreme Winters
Winters in Yakutia are so harsh that people rarely step outside. Birds have been known to freeze mid-flight, and electronic devices can stop functioning due to extreme cold.

People often leave their cars running even during short stops to prevent them from freezing.

### Connectivity and Tourism
Yakutsk has no railway connectivity and relies entirely on air transport. Despite its remoteness, it offers unique attractions such as Kisilyakh Mount, Olekminsk Reserve, Ust-Vilyusky National Park, and Zoo Orto-Doydu.

These places are home to diverse wildlife including bears, moose, deer, Siberian tigers, and various bird species.

## Conclusion
Yakutia is the largest territorial region in the world and one of the coldest inhabited places on Earth. Despite extreme conditions and limited agriculture, the region thrives on natural resources and cultural richness.

It remains one of the most unique and unexplored destinations, offering a blend of natural beauty, extreme climate, and fascinating traditions.
