## Chinese Birthday Celebrations

Birthday is one such day that everybody waits for a whole year. But, do you know that Chinese birthday celebrations are not the same as those of Westerners? Chinese Birthdays still revolve around the time-honored traditions and rituals that are followed over many years. Apart from the traditions, there are certain taboos that one needs to be aware of. If you have a Chinese friend and you are planning to celebrate his/her birthday, then stick to the end to learn some interesting facts about Chinese Birthday celebrations.  

We will also be discussing some of the important birthday dates, gifts, and food. Stay Tuned!

## Chinese Birthday

Do you know – the Chinese usually celebrate the birthdays of a baby or an old person? Young people normally stray away from birthday celebrations.  

A baby’s birthday celebration is considered an important affair hence, parents make sure to throw a grand birthday dinner or banquet for the baby.

Chinese don't celebrate a baby’s first birthday, but the second. This is because the Chinese believe that a baby is already one year old at the time of birth hence, they usually celebrate the second birthday which in western culture is the first birthday of a baby.

## Future Predictions

On the second birthday celebration of a baby, there are certain things done to predict the future of the baby. A baby is surrounded by different kinds of materials and gifts, and whatever baby picks the first move towards is said to be the deciding factor for the future of the baby.

For example:

- If a baby runs towards money, then it is said that the baby is going to be very rich in the future. They are money minded and will do well professionally.  
- If a baby picks up the doll then it is believed that he/she would have a lot of kids in the future.  
- If the child picks up the toy airplane, then the baby is destined to travel the world as an airplane signifies traveling.  

## Important Birthday Dates

Apart from the first birthday, there are certain important dates that the Chinese keep a track of:

- **30** – The age of 30 is said to be dangerous and bad for a girl. Hence, a woman doesn’t celebrate their 30th birthday instead they remain 29 for a year more. And if someone asks them about their age, they simply say, "I am remaining 29 for a year more."  
- **33** – 33 is yet another number that women fear. On a 33rd birthday, women do a ritual to cast off evil. A Chinese woman would buy meat on her 33rd birthday and would stand behind the kitchen door and hack the meat 33 times. This is the way to cast off evil and bad luck.  
- **66** – 66th birthday is the same as the 33rd birthday, where a woman takes the help of a daughter or a relative to repeat the ritual performed on the 33rd birthday. Instead of 33 pieces, this time the meat is cut down into 66 pieces.  

Now, if we talk about men, their 40th birthday is considered inauspicious or bad luck. Hence, men usually stay 39 for a year more and skip their 40th birthday. Oftentimes, men directly celebrate their 60th birthday skipping all the ones in the between.

## When to Celebrate a Birthday?

Chinese birthdays are an affair of rituals and traditions; hence, people take very good care in keeping their traditions alive. Therefore, being fully aware of Chinese birthday traditions is an important thing.  

For example:

- People celebrate their birthday on the day itself, not after the birthday.  
- Hence, if you forgot your Chinese friend’s birthday, then don’t bother to wish them belated birthdays. As Chinese people believe that birthdays are a constant reminder of mortality. Hence, it is considered rude and negligent on one’s part for wishing someone after a birthday.  
- They think that it is impolite and bad to remind someone that one more year is detected from their life and how they are approaching the end.  
- That’s why; if someone skips the celebration or forgot to wish then they don’t wish others belated birthdays.

## Birthday Cake

Well, one of the most exciting things about a birthday celebration is the birthday cake. But, Chinese don't order birthday cakes but cherish longevity noodles. Longevity noodles symbolize long life. Yes, you read it right. Longevity noodles are a birthday delicacy for the Chinese.  

- The longevity noodles are prepared by mixing together the egg, wheat flour, and soda water. The mixture gives a spongy texture which makes the noodles soft and slippery.  
- While preparing the noodles, they should not be broken. The birthday boy or girl should slurp the noodles whole without breaking them off. Breaking off the noodle is believed to be a bad omen which means the life of the person is cut short. Hence, people are expected to consume the longevity noodles whole and in one go.  

Apart from the longevity noodles, there are certain other dishes that you will find at every birthday celebration:

- The red dyed eggs symbolize joy and renewed life.  
- Dumplings represent good fortune and happiness.

## Birthday Gifts

A birthday gift is something that captures the attention of the family and relatives. In China, birthday gifts hold a special place in society. It decides the value and stature of a person in society. Hence, people make sure to display the amount of the gift purchased clearly so that the other person can understand the intention and thought process behind the gift.  

- The most ideal gift as per the Chinese traditions is the red envelope which is also called **“红包 hóng bāo.”** The red envelope consists of a certain amount of money in it. This is considered the most convenient form of gift as money will allow the person to purchase things as per his/her desire.  
- In the case of children, parents or relatives or the attendees of the celebration usually take the kid to the shop to purchase things according to them. This is another method to keep things transparent with the birthday person.  
- Gifts are generally not wrapped as happens in western culture. People give the gift without wrapping it in colorful gift wrappers.  

## Taboo Gift Choices

1. **Shoes** – While westerners believe in giving useful items as gifts, some of these gift items are taboo for your Chinese friend. Shoes are at the top of the list, shoes in Chinese sound like evil. Hence, people avoid giving them as gifts.  
2. **Watches and clocks** – Well, if you want to offend your Chinese friend, then, buy them a watch or a clock. As these items are a constant reminder of how time is running fast. This lets people think about mortality and how death is approaching people. This should not be given as a gift, especially to elderly people.  
3. **Candles** – Candles are considered the item of funeral or mourning rituals. Hence, this isn’t the ideal birthday gift.  
4. **Necklaces and belts** – These items are considered intimate gift items and are said to be gifted to one's partner or better half. Hence, people avoid giving them to friends or family members.  

Another interesting fact about birthday gifts is that people tend to politely refuse to take a gift at first. But, this should not stop one from insisting again. Upon insistence, people accept the gifts from the giver.
