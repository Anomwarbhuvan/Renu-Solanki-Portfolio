## “We travel not to escape life, but for life not to escape us.”

Are you one of those who wants to fill their pockets with the wealth of experiences? Do you
spend most of your hours daydreaming about spending your vacation in some exotic
destination? If yes, then congratulations, you are born with the heart of a traveler.

Do you remember the last time you went on vacation? Do you remember how you felt? The feel
of grass beneath your feet, the warmth of the sky filling your heart, the love you felt when you
bid goodbye to the place, and finally, when you were returning, the flood of emotions that
brought you peace. But along with this, you also felt a tightness in your stomach for spending
far more than you had planned.

We all search for reasons to escape our day-to-day lives; it could be the routine from home
to office, the feeling of stress or sadness that quietly settles within you, or maybe the desire
to set out on a solo journey.

Yet, at times, you end up calling your plan off. Most of you cancel your plans for one
particular reason that almost everyone has faced: the budget. When your expenses exceed your
expectations, you end up canceling your trip.

What if I told you there is a solution to this universal problem? To be precise, the solution
to this common financial concern lies within your planning.

While planning any trip, most of you focus more on accommodation than any other aspect of
traveling. You either look for a luxurious hotel or resort or end up spending a huge amount on
bookings. While you concentrate heavily on hotels, more than half of your budget disappears
in the blink of an eye.

Instead, I would suggest not emptying your pockets on lavish hotels. Choose **homestays** or
**bed & breakfast** options. This way, you get the opportunity to interact with locals,
understand the culture more deeply, and keep your budget under control.

You can achieve all this by setting aside a small portion of your salary. Save a little amount
every month for a memorable trip full of adventure, love, and fun. I am sure this approach
will help you plan your next trip with ease.
